Beliefs & Mental Models

A short list. Updated as I change my mind.

  • Most confusion is a naming problem in disguise.
  • The person who asks "why" twice understands more than the one who asked once and stopped.
  • Rigor and wonder are not opposites — they are the same impulse, facing different directions.
  • Speed is usually the enemy of understanding; slowness is usually the enemy of doing. Both traps are real.
  • If you can't explain it without the jargon, you don't know it yet.
  • Systems that reward performance tend to destroy the love of the thing being performed.
  • Boredom is information. It means you need harder problems, not easier distractions.
  • Almost every skill compounds. The question is whether you started.
